# Namaste Spa — Instagram Insight Layer

**Source:** https://www.instagram.com/namastedayspa/
**Date:** 2026-06-21
**Why this matters:** The Instagram and the website describe **two different businesses**. The website is a 2014 fossil. The Instagram is the *real, current, thriving* Namaste. The gap between them is the single most important strategic finding in this whole audit.

---

## 1. The numbers — this is a *successful* business

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Followers | **~12.5K–13K** |
| Posts | **1,704** |
| Following | ~2,250 |
| Category | Beauty, cosmetic & personal care |

**13K followers and 1,700+ posts** is not a sleepy local spa — it's an established, active, well-followed brand with a real audience and years of consistent content. Which makes the 2014 website even more damning: **the website is dramatically under-serving a business that is clearly thriving everywhere else.** The brand outgrew its website years ago and never updated it.

---

## 2. The brand voice — it already exists, and it's good

The Instagram bio:

> **Wellness • Beauty • Balance 🌸**
> **Sherwood Park's sanctuary for self-care & healing.**
> **✨ Book your peace → namastespa.ca**

This is *the* finding. The brand already has a warm, modern, confident voice:
- *"Sanctuary for self-care & healing"* — emotional, evocative, on-trend.
- *"Book your peace"* — a beautiful, ownable CTA. This line alone is better than every word on the current homepage.
- The tone is calm, human, a little poetic — exactly the spa voice the website completely lacks.

**The redesign doesn't need to invent a voice — it needs to bring the Instagram voice onto the website.** Right now the site sounds like a 2014 WordPress theme; the Instagram sounds like a 2026 spa. "Book your peace" should arguably be the hero line of the new site.

---

## 3. The *actual* service menu has evolved — toward medical aesthetics

Instagram Story Highlights (what they're actively promoting *now*):

`SPA BTS · Retail Boutique · Qi Beauty · Slimwave technology · Massage Therapy · Microneedling · Dermaplaning · Microderm`

This is a **meaningful shift from what the website foregrounds.** The site leads with traditional day-spa/ayurveda framing. The Instagram shows the business has moved toward **medical aesthetics & advanced skincare**: microneedling, dermaplaning, microdermabrasion, Slimwave body contouring, Qi Beauty. These are higher-margin, higher-intent, trend-forward treatments — and they're the things the business is choosing to spotlight today.

**Implication for the rebuild:** the new site's services architecture should reflect the *current* business — advanced skin/aesthetics + body contouring promoted alongside the classic spa/massage/wellness offering — not the 2014 service mix frozen in the old nav.

---

## 4. The competitive set — Instagram tells us who they're really up against

Instagram's "Related accounts" are almost entirely **medical aesthetics / medi-spas**, mostly in the Edmonton (YEG) / Alberta area:

> The Face Institute (Medical Aesthetics) · Lucere (Injectables & Laser) · Enhanced Medical Aesthetics · DermaLux Medical Aesthetics · Freya MediSpa · Nisa MediSpa · Beauty Diamond MediSpa · Nakatsui DermaSurgery · EIE MediSpa · Rose Wellness Centre · Therapeutic Body Concepts …

**Why this matters:** the algorithm has classified Namaste alongside **premium medical-aesthetics clinics**, not budget day spas. That's the league they're actually competing in. Those competitors almost universally have **polished, modern, photography-led, conversion-focused websites.** Against that field, the current 2014 Namaste site isn't just dated — it's a *competitive liability*. A prospective client comparing Namaste to Lucere or The Face Institute online will see a decade-old gap in credibility before they read a word.

This raises the stakes of the rebuild from "nice to have" to "table stakes to compete in their own market."

---

## 5. Assets we now know exist (and should pull into the rebuild)

The Instagram is a ready-made content library the website ignores:
- **1,700+ posts** of real treatment photography, before/afters, the space, the team, products — far better than the stock/celebrity imagery on the site.
- **An active reviews/testimonial stream** in comments and tags (social proof the website links out to instead of showing).
- **Proven captions / voice** to source real copy from — no need to write the brand from scratch.
- **A clear "currently trending here" signal** (the Highlights) for what to feature on the homepage.

When we do the assets pass, the Instagram grid — not the old WordPress media library — is the source of truth for photography and tone.

---

## 6. The sharpened strategic conclusion

Combining the website audit with the Instagram:

> **Namaste is a successful, modern, medical-aesthetics-leaning wellness brand with ~13K followers and a strong voice — trapped behind a 2014 website that represents a business it no longer is.**

The redesign brief gets clearer and more confident because of this:
- **Voice:** already exists on Instagram ("sanctuary for self-care & healing," "book your peace") — port it over wholesale.
- **Positioning:** premium wellness + medical aesthetics, competing with polished YEG medi-spas — design to *that* standard.
- **Content:** lead the homepage with the treatments they actually promote now (skin/aesthetics + body contouring + signature massage/head spa), not the frozen 2014 menu.
- **Proof:** surface the 13K-strong social presence and real reviews directly on the site.
- **Imagery:** source from the Instagram grid — real, warm, on-brand — and retire the stock/celebrity hero.

The website isn't behind on *design*. It's behind on *reality*. The job is to make the site finally tell the truth about how good this business already is.
